This file contains the complete statistical results of the experiments.
The refinement results are reported for the best mutation obtained in Stage 1. Using the BBOB benchmark within the IOHexperimenter environment, each refined variant was evaluated over 30 independent runs with a budget of 100,000 function evaluations per run. Results are presented for the 40-dimensional setting, as it represents the most challenging scenario and provides a more discriminative assessment of scalability and robustness.

The table presents the AB metric, defined as the average of the best fitness values obtained across the 30 independent runs. Each LLM corresponds to a mutation operator generated by that model. To facilitate comparison, the three best results for each function are highlighted as follows: first place in bold, second place in blue, and third place in red.

The results show that the refinement process achieves clear improvements over the baseline operator, increasing the number of functions with lower minimum fitness values. In particular, the mutation refined using GPT-5 mini demonstrates the best overall performance, achieving the top results in 12 out of the 24 BBOB functions and showing consistent and robust improvements across the benchmark.

Overall, these findings confirm that iterative refinement guided by function-wise comparisons is an effective strategy to strengthen mutation operators and improve convergence behavior within the Differential Evolution framework.
